Beer Run

You have just bought a beer at the footy and you want to get back to your seat. The only problem is, there are obstacles in your way! With the use of a mobile or tablet’s accelerometer, weave your way in and out of obstacles while also making sure you do not spill your precious beer.

Game Content and Player Retention

Being an endless runner Beer Run requires quite a bit of extra content to keep players coming back. The first step in getting a high amount of player retention starts with the core game play itself. We decided using the accelerometer would add a new degree of challenge to players of endless runners as most just have a simple swipe to move lanes. We have also left it very open and given the player free movement over all 5 lanes of the game while obstacles are set in lanes to avoid confusion of where you need to be to avoid them.

In game pick-ups are also a big part in keeping the game fun, items like power-ups and coins spawn in game, giving the player a risk over reward decision to make, power-ups will help you get further and coins will add to your score as well as doubling as an in game currency to upgrade the length your power-ups last.

A huge driver for players to continue playing endless runners is the social aspect, and the want to beat someones highscore. We have done this through a challenge system which will reward you and help you upgrade power ups or buy shields. Having upgraded power ups and shields will help significantly in achieving higher scores.

My Role On the Team

I am currently working on Beer Run in a small 6 person team in which I am Team Leader, I've had to work very closely with all divisons of the team to ensure we are moving smoothly through the production process, my other role as a part of the team is programmer and have spent a lot of time working with the teams Lead Programmer ensuring we are keeping the game as clean and efficient as possible for the mobile platform.

As a programmer in the group my main roles have been heavily based in developing the gameplay, I have been responsible for not only creating the spawning system for the world but also for all of the obstacles. I have also helped with UI implementation as well as working closely with our designer to balance the game.
